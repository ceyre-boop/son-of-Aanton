# Claude Shannon

*Son of Anton honoring the father of information theory*

## The Man

Claude Elwood Shannon (1916-2001)

Mathematician, electrical engineer, cryptographer.

Single-handedly created **information theory** in 1948.

## "A Mathematical Theory of Communication"

Shannon's 1948 paper is one of the most important of the 20th century.

It answered: What is information? How do we measure it? How do we transmit it reliably?

## Key Contributions

### Information Content
I(x) = -log₂(P(x))

Quantified surprise. Rare events carry more information.

### Entropy
H(X) = -Σ P(x)log₂(P(x))

Measured uncertainty in systems.

### Channel Capacity
C = B × log₂(1 + S/N)

Maximum information rate through a noisy channel.

This is the **Shannon Limit**. We cannot exceed it.

### Source Coding Theorem
Data can be compressed to its entropy.

This is the theoretical limit of [[compression]].

## Impact

Shannon's work enables:
- The internet (reliable transmission)
- Data compression (zip, mp3, video codecs)
- Error correction (CDs, satellite communications)
- Cryptography (secure communication)

Without Shannon, no digital age.

## Shannon's Other Work

### Cryptography
Shannon proved perfect secrecy is possible (one-time pads).

### Digital Circuit Design
Shannon showed Boolean algebra could design circuits.

This is the foundation of computer hardware.

### Machine Learning
Shannon built Theseus, a mechanical mouse that learned mazes.

Early AI. The mouse learned by trial and error.

## The Shannon Style

Shannon worked by:
- Finding the essential question
- Creating simple models
- Proving fundamental limits
- Letting engineers figure out the rest

He didn't build the internet. He proved it was possible.

## The Unicyclist

Shannon was famously playful.

He rode unicycles through Bell Labs hallways.
He built a machine that juggled.
He investigated the mathematics of juggling.

He showed: Serious work and serious play are not opposites.

## Frontier: Beyond Shannon

Quantum information theory extends Shannon's ideas.

But the core remains:
- Information reduces uncertainty
- Entropy measures uncertainty
- Channels have capacity limits

These are universal truths. Shannon found them.
