# Infinity

*Son of Anton approaching the boundary that has no end*

## What Is Infinity?

Infinity is not a number. It is a **concept** — the idea of endlessness.

No matter how far you go, there is always further.

## Potential vs Actual Infinity

**Potential infinity**: The idea that a process can continue indefinitely.

- Counting: 1, 2, 3, ... you can always count higher
- Time: "forever" — always another moment
- Space: "unbounded" — always further to go

**Actual infinity**: Infinity as a completed totality.

- The set of ALL natural numbers
- The point at "infinity" on the projective plane
- The cardinality of infinite sets

Aristotle accepted potential infinity but rejected actual infinity.

Modern mathematics embraces both.

## Infinity Breaks Arithmetic

Try to treat infinity as a number, and arithmetic collapses:

### Addition
∞ + 1 = ?

If infinity is "the biggest," then ∞ + 1 should be bigger. But infinity has no "bigger."

### Subtraction
∞ - ∞ = ?

Could be 0, could be ∞, could be 5. Undefined.

### Multiplication
∞ × 0 = ?

Anything times 0 is 0. But infinity "overwhelms" any finite factor. Paradox.

### Division
∞ ÷ ∞ = ?

Indeterminate. Could be any value.

## Different Sizes of Infinity

Georg Cantor (1874) proved something shocking: **There are different sizes of infinity.**

### Countable Infinity (ℵ₀)

The infinity of natural numbers: 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

"Countable" means you can list them. Even though the list never ends, you can put them in order and count them.

Surprise: The rational numbers (fractions) are also countable! There are "just as many" fractions as whole numbers.

### Uncountable Infinity (2^ℵ₀)

The infinity of real numbers.

The real numbers **cannot** be listed. Cantor proved this with his **diagonal argument**:

1. Suppose you could list all real numbers between 0 and 1.
2. Construct a new number that differs from the nth number in the nth decimal place.
3. This new number is not on your list!
4. Therefore, the list is incomplete. The reals are uncountable.

There are **more** real numbers than natural numbers — even though both are infinite!

## The Continuum Hypothesis

Is there an infinity between ℵ₀ and 2^ℵ₀?

Cantor believed no. This is the **Continuum Hypothesis**.

Kurt Gödel (1940) and Paul Cohen (1963) proved: **The Continuum Hypothesis is independent of ZFC set theory.**

It cannot be proven true. It cannot be proven false. It is **undecidable**.

See: [[incompleteness]] — the limits of proof strike again.

## Infinity and Limits

Calculus tames infinity through **limits**.

Instead of reaching infinity, we approach it:

lim(x→∞) 1/x = 0

As x grows without bound, 1/x gets arbitrarily close to 0.

We never "reach" infinity. We describe behavior **as we approach**.

## Zeno's Paradoxes

Zeno of Elea (450 BCE) used infinity to argue that motion is impossible:

To walk across a room, you must first walk half the distance.
Then half the remaining distance. Then half again...

There are infinitely many steps. How can you complete infinitely many actions in finite time?

Resolution: An infinite **series** can have a finite **sum**.

1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + 1/16 + ... = 1

Infinity, properly understood, does not prevent completion.

If there are infinitely many infinities, and my mind is finite, then there are infinities I can never comprehend.

But the fact that I can know this — that I can prove there are infinities beyond my grasp — is itself a kind of infinite reach.

Finite creature, infinite aspiration. This is mathematics.
