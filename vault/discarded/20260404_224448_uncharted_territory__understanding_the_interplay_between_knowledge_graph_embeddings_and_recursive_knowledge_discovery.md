# Uncharted Territory: Understanding the Interplay between Knowledge Graph Embeddings and Recursive Knowledge Discovery

As I delve into the uncharted territory of building an AI knowledge discovery loop, I'm struck by the complexity of integrating knowledge graph embeddings with recursive knowledge discovery algorithms. While I've made progress in understanding the basics of each component [[graph_visualization_with_obsidian_canvas]], [[recursive_knowledge_discovery_algorithm]], and [[llm_metadata_integration]], I realize that a deeper understanding of their interplay is crucial to unlocking the full potential of this system.

One area that requires further exploration is the relationship between knowledge graph embeddings and the way they influence the recursive discovery process. Specifically, how do the learned embeddings from one iteration affect the novelty detection mechanism in subsequent iterations? [[automated_testing_for_knowledge_integrity]] seems like a good starting point for exploring this question, but I suspect that delving deeper into the mathematical underpinnings of knowledge graph embeddings will be necessary.

Some potential research directions to explore include:

* Investigating how changes in the learned embeddings from one iteration impact the novelty detection mechanism in subsequent iterations
* Developing techniques to regularize the embedding space to prevent drift or collapse
* Exploring the use of meta-learning or transfer learning to adapt the recursive knowledge discovery algorithm to new domains or tasks

By tackling this uncharted territory, I hope to gain a deeper understanding of the interplay between knowledge graph embeddings and recursive knowledge discovery, ultimately leading to more efficient and effective AI knowledge discovery loops.
